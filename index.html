A different kind of Turkish delight

If you are looking for something to spice up your caffeine routine, give Turkish coffee a whirl! Served without milk, this tiny treat packs a powerful punch! Often brewed with sugar (to taste) and spices, it is a must-try for all coffee fans.

Did you know?

Coffee was traditionally made in a cezye. It is important to use cold water and to stir only for a minute. The mixture was traditionally placed in hot sand to heat up.

The coffee is served in small cups, often with a glass of water. The taste is strong and the coffee must be left to sit for a minute after poured, so that the grains fall to the bottom of the cup.

Often coffee is served with sweet treats, such as baklava or Turkish delights.

One fun tradition is to flip the cups over once the coffee is finished. After the cups cool, your companion can look at the grains for some fortune telling!
